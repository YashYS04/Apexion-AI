=== FIA FORMULA ONE SPORTING REGULATIONS - EXCERPTS FOR APEXION AI ===

ARTICLE 24: TYRES AND WHEELS
24.1 tyre Compounds:
    a) Pirelli will provide three dry-weather compounds for each Competition: Hard (White banding), Medium (Yellow banding), and Soft (Red banding).
    b) Each driver is allocated 13 sets of dry-weather tyres, 4 sets of intermediate tyres, and 3 sets of wet-weather tyres for each race weekend.
24.2 Compulsory Compound Usage during the Race:
    a) Unless wet-weather conditions are declared by the Race Director (in which case wet or intermediate tyres must be used), each driver must use at least two different specifications/compounds of dry-weather tyres during the race.
    b) At least one of these two compulsory sets must be a set of the mandatory race tyres designated by Pirelli (usually the Hard or Medium compound).
    c) Failure to comply with this requirement will result in exclusion from the race results or a post-race 30-second time penalty.
24.3 Tyre Degradation and Safety:
    a) Operating a tyre beyond its structural degradation limit (typically defined when tread depth is less than 1.0mm or wear exceeds 90%) poses a catastrophic risk of tyre delamination or puncture.
    b) In the event of high thermal degradation (overheating), tyre compound temperature exceeding 135°C (surface) or 115°C (carcass) will lead to severe loss of friction coefficient and accelerated wear rate.

ARTICLE 34: PIT ENTRY, PIT LANE, AND PIT EXIT
34.1 Speed Limit:
    a) A speed limit of 80km/h will be enforced in the pit lane during the entire Event. Under specific tight tracks (e.g., Monaco, Singapore), this limit may be reduced to 60km/h by the Race Director.
    b) Exceeding this limit during the race will incur a 5-second or 10-second time penalty, or a drive-through penalty.
34.2 Pit Stop Delta Time:
    a) The total pit lane transit time (the time lost entering, slowing down, stopping, and exiting) is known as the "pit lane delta".
    b) At Silverstone, the pit lane delta is approximately 20.5 seconds under green flag conditions. At Monaco, it is approximately 25.0 seconds. At Spa-Francorchamps, it is approximately 22.0 seconds.
    c) Under Virtual Safety Car (VSC) or Safety Car (SC) conditions, the pit lane delta is significantly reduced relative to cars on track (often by 10-12 seconds) due to the reduced speed limit enforced on the main track, making pit stops under VSC/SC highly advantageous (the "cheap pit stop" effect).

ARTICLE 55: SAFETY CAR AND VIRTUAL SAFETY CAR (VSC)
55.1 Safety Car Procedure:
    a) When the Safety Car is deployed, all competing cars must reduce speed and line up behind it.
    b) No car may overtake another car on track, except under specific circumstances (e.g., a car slowing with an obvious problem).
    c) Lapped cars may be permitted to unlap themselves if the Race Director deems it safe.
55.2 Virtual Safety Car (VSC):
    a) The VSC may be deployed when double waved yellow flags are needed, but the hazard does not warrant the Safety Car itself.
    b) When VSC is deployed, drivers must reduce speed and stay above the minimum sector time (delta time) set by the FIA telemetry system.
    c) Pit stops are permitted under VSC. Since cars on the main track are restricted to a slow target delta, pit stops under VSC save substantial race position time. The effective time loss is reduced by approximately 45%.

ARTICLE 12: WET WEATHER CONDITIONS
12.1 Wet Track Declarations:
    a) If it starts to rain, the Race Director may declare a "Wet Track".
    b) Once a Wet Track is declared, the DRS (Drag Reduction System) is disabled, and drivers are permitted to use intermediate or wet tyres without needing to run two different dry-weather compounds.
    c) Intermediate tyres (Green) are optimal for damp tracks with no standing water. Full wet tyres (Blue) are designed for heavy rain and standing water to prevent aquaplaning.
